What Can You
Trade in Futures?
Five major categories — each with its own behaviour and characteristics
▶ Equity Index Futures
NQ · ES · YM · RTY  |  Micros: MNQ · MES · MYM · M2K
Trade the overall movement of the US stock market. Micros are 1/10th the contract size — perfect for smaller accounts & beginners.
▶ Commodity Futures
CL (Crude Oil) · GC (Gold) · SI (Silver) · NG (Natural Gas)
Trade raw materials. Heavily influenced by geopolitical events, supply/demand data, and macro news cycles.
▶ Currency / Forex Futures
6E (Euro) · 6B (GBP) · 6J (Yen) · 6A (AUD)
Trade major currency pairs via CME — regulated, centralized, no broker requoting. Cleaner fills than spot forex.
▶ Interest Rate / Bond Futures
ZB (30yr Bond) · ZN (10yr Note) · ZF (5yr Note)
Driven by Fed policy, inflation data, and Treasury auctions. Correlated with equity indices on risk-off days.
▶ Crypto Futures (CME)
BTC · ETH · MBT (Micro Bitcoin) · MET (Micro Ether)
Regulated crypto exposure via CME. Cash settled. Same volatility, no wallet/custody risk.

What is
Contract Rollover?

Futures contracts have an expiration date. When that date approaches, traders "roll over" to the next contract — closing the current position and opening the same trade in the newer, further-dated contract. Volume is your signal.

01
Contracts have a fixed expiry
E.g. NQ June 2026 expires on the 3rd Friday of June 2026. Trading ends, cash settlement begins.
02
Volume shifts ~1 week before expiry
Open interest and volume in the current contract drops sharply. The next contract starts receiving all the flow.
03
Price gaps appear on non-continuous charts
If you're on NQM26 (specific contract), you'll see a gap when the new contract opens. Always use NQ1! on TradingView.
